Ontario Workforce Priority (OWP) Stream: The Complete Guide (July 2026)

Provincial Nominee Programs July 2026
← Back to Blog

What Changed

Ontario eliminated all eight former OINP streams and replaced them with one new stream, the Ontario Workforce Priority (OWP) Stream, effective June 26, 2026 — the biggest structural overhaul in OINP's history (Phase 1 of a two-phase redesign). It covers job offers across every TEER level (0 through 5) through three pathways: TEER 0–3, TEER 4–5, and self-employed physicians. All pathways require a full-time, indeterminate (permanent) Ontario job offer except the physician pathway. (For the story of how the old streams were closed, see our June article on the OINP overhaul.)

Eligibility Requirements

Within each pathway, job offer, education, and language requirements all apply together (AND) — every one of them must be met. Work experience is the one exception: applicants only need to satisfy one of several listed options (OR).

General Job Offer & Employer Requirements (All Pathways Except Physicians)

  • Job offer: full-time (30+ hours/week), indeterminate duration, wage at or above the prevailing median for the occupation (via Job Bank), and for work that occurs primarily in Ontario
  • Employer: operating in Ontario for at least 3 years, meets minimum revenue and staffing thresholds (below), no outstanding Employment Standards Act or Occupational Health and Safety Act orders
  • Ownership: neither the applicant nor family members may hold 10%+ equity in the employer's business

Who Counts as a "Recent Ontario Graduate"?

This status matters because it unlocks three separate benefits at once: the reduced 3-month work-experience option, a full exemption from the language requirement, and the lower "low-wage" threshold instead of the regional median wage. Under O. Reg. 204/26, a recent Ontario graduate is someone who, within the last three years, obtained one of the following credentials from an eligible Ontario post-secondary institution:

  • A two-year (or longer) full-time degree or diploma
  • An Ontario college graduate certificate
  • A master's degree
  • A PhD
Important: A one-year Ontario diploma or certificate does not, on its own, meet the "recent graduate" definition — it must be at least a two-year credential, a college graduate certificate, or a graduate degree. This status is only available under the TEER 0–3 pathway.

TEER 4–5 Pathway — Essential Workers

All of the following are required — there is no alternative work-experience option here, and no "recent graduate" relief:

RequirementDetails
Job offerFull-time, permanent Ontario job offer in a TEER 4 or 5 occupation (open to any TEER 4–5 occupation — a major broadening vs. the old In-Demand Skills stream's fixed list)
LanguageCLB 4, all four skills — new requirement (the old In-Demand Skills stream had no language floor)
EducationCanadian secondary school diploma or equivalent
WageRegional median wage for the occupation
Work experience9 months cumulative in the last 2 years, specifically in the job-offer position with the job-offer employer — no outside-employer option

Self-Employed Physicians

No job offer needed. Required: active membership in good standing with the College of Physicians and Surgeons of Ontario (CPSO), a valid certificate of registration (independent, academic, or provisional class), and eligibility to bill through OHIP.

Wage Calculation — Clarified

The job offer wage must meet or exceed the prevailing median wage for that occupation and region, as published by Job Bank Canada, assessed at application time — it's a pass/fail threshold, not a sliding scale. It must also be at least equal to whatever the employer is currently paying the applicant in that role, if applicable.

The only exception is recent Ontario graduates under the TEER 0–3 pathway: they can qualify at a separate, lower "low-wage" level instead of the full regional median. This exception is tied specifically to recent-graduate status (defined above) — it doesn't apply to TEER 4–5 workers or to TEER 0–3 applicants who qualify through the 6-month or 2-year experience options.

Express Entry vs. Non-Express Entry Nomination

Once approved, applicants can choose how their nomination is used — but the choice isn't open to everyone:

  • TEER 0–3 pathway and self-employed physicians: applicants who hold a valid Express Entry profile at the time of nomination can choose to be nominated through IRCC's Express Entry system, which routes them into an Express Entry invitation round for Provincial Nominee candidates and generally means faster federal processing. If they don't choose this route (or don't have an EE profile), the nomination proceeds through the standard non-Express Entry PR application process instead.
  • TEER 4–5 pathway: Ontario's guidance lists the Express Entry option only for TEER 0–3 and physician applicants. TEER 4–5 nominees go through the non-Express Entry PR process only.
  • If you accept an Express Entry nomination, you receive an additional 600 points toward your CRS score, which puts you into the next Express Entry invitation round. You then have 60 days to submit your PR application to IRCC.

EOI Scoring — Official Grid (Published July 20, 2026)

Ontario posted the full EOI scoring grid on ontario.ca on July 20, 2026. Every EOI you register gets scored against the factors below, broken out here by category so you can see where your points actually come from. If you're invited to apply, you'll need documents backing up whatever you claimed.

One thing worth flagging: these points are separate from the minimum eligibility rules earlier in this guide. Meeting the eligibility bar doesn't earn you points, and scoring well doesn't excuse you from the eligibility bar. You need both.

The new grid generally weights work experience, wage, language, and regional location more heavily than the old streams did, and adds more granular tiers. Total EOI cutoff scores for invitations have not been announced — those will emerge once the first OWP draws take place.

Status of Applications & Draw Competitiveness

The EOI portal closed June 25, 2026 and hasn't reopened yet — expected sometime this summer (July–August), though no firm date has been set. Pending EOIs that hadn't yet received an invitation to apply are being automatically withdrawn by the province; applications already submitted before June 26 are grandfathered under the old rules. Once the EOI system reopens, everyone registers a fresh profile — old scores and profiles do not carry over.

Worth tempering expectations: of Ontario's 14,119 total nomination allocation for 2026, roughly 13,278 had already been invited to apply earlier in the year under the old streams before they closed — leaving only about 6–10% of the year's allocation for the new OWP stream's remaining draws.

Important: Broader eligibility does not necessarily mean better odds of an invitation.

What's Next: Phase 2

Ontario has signaled a second phase adding three more streams — a Priority Healthcare Stream (no job offer required), a redesigned Entrepreneur Stream, and an Exceptional Talent Stream (assessed qualitatively rather than by job offer or points). These are proposed only: no regulation, eligibility criteria, or launch date has been confirmed.
